OK, not just yet...

Of course, we're never going to charge you to use The Public Whip. It would kind of defeat the point. We're offended that you thought otherwise. At the moment, the site is extremely cheap to run. But that could change.

Last night the EU Council of Ministers adopted legislation which would make our costs a lots higher. They did so despite opposition from several countries, and the elected European Parliament. And they had to break their own procedure to adopt it. The new law is not only bad law, but the way it is being passed is anti-democratic.

Please read this excellent talk to find out why we disagree with the new law. But here's a quick summary:

  • The new law allows somebody else to own intellectual property that we made.
  • It makes it costly for us to even find out that somebody else owns it. That's the £N000 charge mentioned on the previous page; we'd have to shell out for an initial basic search by a patent lawyer.
  • The "intellectual property" concerned isn't even very clever. It's about as complex as how to lock a front door, or make a piece of toast.

What we'd like you to do

  • Write to all your MEPs and tell them to vote against the directive when it comes back to the European Parliament for second reading. If you're not sure what to put, this ZDNet leader will give you some inspiration.
  • That's all. We're not feeling demanding today.
